Get started

Two things. About three minutes.

No coding. The voice runs on your Mac, so setup is a one-time install — then it just works, even without Wi-Fi.

1

Add Spiel to Chrome

One click from the Chrome Web Store — no developer mode, no zip files.

Add to Chrome — free
Then pin the Spiel icon to your toolbar. Prefer to install manually? Guide →
2

Install the voice

Paste this one line into the Terminal app and press Enter. It sets up the local AI voice.

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/preet01/spiel/main/install.sh | bash
~2 minutes. When your Mac says “Spiel is ready” out loud, you're done.
3

Press Play

Open any article or PDF, click the Spiel icon, and hit Play. Words highlight as it reads — click anywhere to jump.

Requires an Apple Silicon Mac (M1 or newer). Intel & Windows are on the roadmap.

Privacy

Nothing you read ever leaves your computer.

Most read-aloud tools stream your reading history through their servers. Spiel doesn't have servers. The voice engine listens only on 127.0.0.1 — your own machine, unreachable from the network.

The only time Spiel touches the internet is the one-time download of the voice model at install.

Questions

Good to know

Is it really free?

Yes — completely. Spiel is open-source (MIT) and there's no account, trial, or paid tier. It's built as a free alternative to subscription read-aloud apps, especially for students and researchers.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. The extension itself is a one-click install from the Chrome Web Store. There's one more step after that: copy one line into the Terminal app (we give you the exact line and a Copy button) and press Enter — that installs the local voice engine, which is what actually makes Spiel talk. No coding involved, just paste and wait ~2 minutes.

Is my data private?

Completely. The AI voice runs entirely on your computer at 127.0.0.1. No text, reading history, or audio is ever sent anywhere. Turn off Wi-Fi and it still works.

What computers does it work on?

Today: Apple Silicon Macs (M1 or newer) with Chrome. Intel Macs and Windows/Linux are on the roadmap — star the repo to follow along.

Can it read PDFs and research papers?

Yes. Spiel extracts the text locally with pdf.js and reads it aloud with the same word-by-word follow-along, for web-hosted and local PDFs alike.
